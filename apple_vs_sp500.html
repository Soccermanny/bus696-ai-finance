🤔 What Does This All Mean?

Returns: Apple made you more money per year (20.64%) compared to the S&P 500's typical 10%. That's impressive!

Volatility (Risk): This measures how bumpy the ride is. Apple's 31.80% volatility means on a typical day, the stock price could swing up or down by about 2%. The S&P 500 at 15% volatility is much calmer - about half as bouncy.

Best and Worst Days: Apple had some wild days - up 15.33% on its best day, down 9.25% on its worst. These extreme swings show why volatility matters - you need strong nerves to hold through the drops!

Think of it like roller coasters: Apple is the intense thrill ride with huge drops and climbs - exciting but scary. The S&P 500 is the family-friendly coaster - still fun, but much smoother. Apple gets you to the end faster (higher returns), but you'll grip the safety bar tighter (higher volatility).

💡 Key Takeaways

  • Higher returns = Higher risk: Apple's 20.64% return came with double the volatility of the S&P 500
  • Diversification matters: The S&P 500 spreads across 500 companies, smoothing out the bumps. Apple is just one company
  • Individual stocks are riskier: Apple's worst day (-9.25%) could wipe out nearly a year of S&P 500 returns in one day
  • Past performance ≠ Future results: These numbers show what happened, not what will happen next
  • Know your risk tolerance: Can you stomach losing 9% in a single day? If not, individual stocks might not be for you
